Gậy Ta Ta Vót

My Staff I Carve Myself
25/07/2012 · Hoàng-Ân
Tiếng Việt
"Đường ta ta đi, gậy ta ta vót: giữa đồi núi trập-trùng"
(Một thiền-sư thời thế-kỷ thứ bẩy tây-lịch)


Tham gì cái miếng đỉnh-chung!
Quý gì cái bã lợi-danh xu, hào!
Túi thơ, bầu rượu, lưng trâu,
Thanh-nhàn, thanh-bạch sạch/dơ chẳng mừng!
Mây trời, gió núi, hoa đồng
Thênh-thang lối hẹp / nhỏ to chuyện đời!
Hiền-nhân Đoan-ngọ tháng Năm,
Tai trâu rửa mãi, chưa rời thị-phi...
Áo tơi, giầy cỏ, gậy con,
Tự đan, tự bện, vót thời gậy ta!
Mở lòng đón rộng bầu trời,
Tạ ơn mặt đất trung-thành dưới chân!
Sá gì phú-quý xa-hoa,
Ngày ngày mời khách tô canh xương rồng...

English
“My road I walk, my staff I carve: among mountain upon mountain”
(A Zen-master of the seventh century A.D.)


Why covet the morsel of cauldrons-and-bells!
Why treasure the dregs of profit and fame, pennies and dimes!
A bag of poems, a gourd of wine, a buffalo's back,
Serene, pure-hearted, neither clean/filthy brings delight!
Clouds of Heaven, mountain wind, flowers of the field
Boundless the narrow path / small and great affairs of Life!
The sage at Fifth-Month Đoan-ngọ,
Washing the buffalo's ears forever, yet scandal will not leave...
Raincoat, grass sandals, a little staff,
Self-woven, self-braided, then my staff I carve!
Open the heart to welcome the wide great sky,
Give thanks to the faithful earth beneath my feet!
What matter riches, rank, extravagant splendor,
Each day inviting guests to a bowl of cactus soup...

MY STAFF I CARVE MYSELF

“my road I walk, my staff I carve: among mountain upon mountain”
(A Zen-master of the seventh century A.D.)


Why covet the morsel of cauldrons-and-bells!
Why treasure the dregs of profit and fame, pennies and dimes!
A bag of poems, a gourd of wine, a buffalo's back,
Serene, pure-hearted, neither clean/filthy brings delight!
Clouds of Heaven, mountain wind, flowers of the field
Boundless the narrow path / small and great affairs of Life!
The sage at Fifth-Month Đoan-ngọ,
Washing the buffalo's ears forever, yet scandal will not leave...
Raincoat, grass sandals, a little staff,
Self-woven, self-braided, then my staff I carve!
Open the heart to welcome the wide great sky,

Each day inviting guests to a bowl of cactus soup...

1
Translator's note“Cauldrons-and-bells” signifies aristocratic wealth, while the washed ears and buffalo evoke the recluses Xu You and Chao Fu rejecting worldly office.
