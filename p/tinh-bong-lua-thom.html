Love of the Fragrant Rice-Blossom
26/07/2007 · Hoàng-Ân
Tiếng Việt
...phù-sa đắp-đỗi hai bờ...anh bên bến bồi, riêng em bên bến lở, ngồi gọi đò ngang...anh đò dọc trôi xuôi...dòng sông quê-hương thơm ngây ký-ức, hoa lục-bình tím ngắt mối tình thơ, chợt hoàng-hôn mây cũng gọi mầu cũ dàn bằng-lăng...nhớ xưa em thích ngòi lá tre, anh làm thơ bằng ngòi bút mực bầu...em yêu mầu mực tím nên anh pha cho vừa nỗi-niềm thương...
...mối tình trẻ thơ ngọt như chuối mật trộn xôi vò, bùi như khoai lùi bếp rạ vườn sau...thương làm sao hương tóc em thơm bồ-kết với bưởi trắng cùi ngây...chợt nhớ khi em dọn ra tỉnh thành như Nguyễn Bính thở-than: "Hôm qua em ra tỉnh về...hương đồng gió nội bay đi ít nhiều..."
...ôi, cánh đồng xưa trắng cánh cò thoải nước mây...nhớ bông lúa thơm hai mùa chiêm...tiếng hò ai lẩy vọng đường trời mây xế tím, cam, hồng...mái tóc xưa giờ như mầu nắng xế thôn xưa...ôi, mối tình bông lúa vẫn còn thơm trong ký-ức đã mịt-mùng hư-ảnh...
...nơi phố-thị đèn xe lóa mắt...chợt chiều nay thèm hương lúa tình xưa...

tình bông lúa thơm

English
...alluvium builds and alters the two banks...You beside the gathering shore, I alone beside the crumbling shore, sitting and calling the cross-river ferry...You, a long-river boat drifting downstream...the Homeland river scents memory into rapture, water-hyacinth flowers violet-deep the Poetry Love, suddenly sunset clouds also call the old color of the crape-myrtle row...remember Once I loved a bamboo-leaf nib, You wrote poetry with an ink-gourd pen...I loved violet ink so You mixed it to the measure of our aching-love...
...childhood Love sweet as honey-bananas mingled with xôi-vò, rich as sweet potatoes buried in the straw-fire behind the garden...how dearly I loved the fragrance of my hair perfumed with bồ-kết and white pomelo flowers, their pithy innocence...suddenly remembering when I moved to the city as Nguyễn Bính lamented: "Yesterday you returned from town...the fragrance of fields and the wind of home have flown away somewhat..."
...oh, the old rice-field whitened with stork-wings at ease through water and clouds...remembering fragrant rice-blossoms of the two chiêm harvests...whose hò-song plucks an echo across the heavenly road, clouds of late day violet, orange, rose...the hair of Once now like the color of declining sunlight over the old village...oh, Love of the rice-blossom remains fragrant within memory long vanished into illusory shadow...
...within the city of dazzling lamps and vehicles...suddenly this afternoon I hunger for the rice-fragrance of Love Once...

Love of the Fragrant Rice-Blossom

...oh, the old rice-field whitened with stork-wings at ease through water and clouds...remembering fragrant rice-blossoms of the two chiêm harvests...whose hò-song plucks an echo across the heavenly road, clouds of late day violet, orange, rose...the hair of Once now like the color of declining sunlight over the old village...oh, Love of the rice-blossom remains fragrant within memory long vanished into illusory shadow...

Translator's noteThe quoted lines echo Nguyễn Bính's "Chân Quê," a lament for village simplicity altered by the city.
