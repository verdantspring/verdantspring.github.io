Sinh Ta Cha Ném Bút Rồng

Birthing Me, Father Cast Down the Dragon-Brush
20/12/2005 · Hoàng-Ân
Tiếng Việt
Sinh ra ta khóc ngất rồi
Sinh ta Mẹ thét động trời rúng mây
Sinh ta Cha ngắt mộng đời
Ta sinh ra giữa chốn đầy hy-sinh

Ta sinh ra giữa vòng xoay trái đất
Trái đất mang ta theo cùng với vòng quay
Là nhập-cuộc khi cuộc-chung-quy ấy
Không đổi-dời với sự có-không-ta

Nhưng: Công Cha, Nghĩa Mẹ, Ân Thầy
Hạt cơm, chữ-nghĩa, máu hồng Tổ-Tiên
Sinh ta Cha ném bút-rồng
Để Ta mai-hậu Cháu-Con đền-bồi

(Mùa Giô Tổ Hùng-Vương, 2005 Tây-Lich.)
English
Born, I wept myself senseless —
Birthing me, Mother cried out — the sky shook, the clouds trembled;
Birthing me, Father cut off the dream of his days —
I was born into a place overflowing with Sacrifice.

I was born into the spinning of the earth —
The earth carries me along inside its turning —
To enter the great Round — that Cycle of all returning —
Unchanged by the presence-or-absence of me —

But: Father's Toil, Mother's Devotion, Teacher's Grace —
Each grain of rice, letters and meaning, the red blood of Ancestors —
Birthing me, Father cast down his Dragon-Brush,
So that I, in days to come — Children, Grandchildren — may repay.

(Season of Giỗ Tổ Hùng-Vương, 2005 Western Calendar.)
Translator's noteGiỗ Tổ Hùng-Vương is the annual death-anniversary commemoration of the Hùng Kings, legendary founders of the Vietnamese nation — a festival of ancestral debt structurally mirroring the poem's theme of repayment across generations.
