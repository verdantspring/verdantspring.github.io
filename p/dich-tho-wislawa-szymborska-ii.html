Dịch Thơ Wislawa Szymborska:
VIEW WITH A GRAIN OF SAND
"The Joy of Writing"

Why does this written doe bound through these written woods?
For a drink of written water from a spring
whose surface will xerox her muffle?
Why does she lift her head? does she hear something?
Perched on four slim legs borrowed from the truth,
she pricks up her ears beneath my fingertips.


Hoàng-Ân phóng-dịch: Hạnh-Nguyện Của Sáng-Tạo

Vì sao con nai hư-ảnh này dong-duổi qua những khu
rừng hư-ảnh này?
Để thấm giọng bằng một ngụm nước từ một con suối
mà bờ mặt sẽ sao-bảng bờ hồng nhung kín-đáo của nàng?
Vì sao nàng ngẩng mặt chăm-chú; phải chăng nàng
nghe được động-tĩnh gì?
Bốn chân gầy, vay-mượn trên sự thật, cưu-mang hiện-sinh mình,
Nàng vểnh tai nghe-ngóng dưới đầu những ngón tay tôi.
English
Translating Wislawa Szymborska:
VIEW WITH A GRAIN OF SAND
"The Joy of Writing"

Why does this written doe bound through these written woods?
For a drink of written water from a spring
whose surface will xerox her muffle?
Why does she lift her head? does she hear something?
Perched on four slim legs borrowed from the truth,
she pricks up her ears beneath my fingertips.


Hoàng-Ân, freely rendered: The Blessed-Vow of Creation

Why does this phantom-image doe bound freely through these
phantom-image woods?
To steep her throat with one sip of water from a brook
whose surface will xerox her discreet, velvety-rose muzzle?
Why does she raise her face so intently; could she have heard
some stirring?
Four slim legs, borrowed upon Truth, bearing her own Existence,
She pricks up her ears listening-watching beneath the tips of my fingers.

(24/02/2013)
Translator's note\"Hạnh-Nguyện\" is a Buddhist term for the meritorious vow of a Bodhisattva — Hoàng-Ân reframes Szymborska's creative act as an act of sacred spiritual aspiration, which is the conceptual heart of her free rendering.
